> Variance = Budget − Actual. Negative variance means spending exceeded budget.

---

## Key Findings

### Over Budget
- **Marketing** is the largest concern, running **$2,500 (25.0%) over budget**. This is the only category with a meaningful overspend and warrants investigation into campaign spend or unplanned initiatives.
- **Sales** came in **$1,500 (5.0%) over budget**, likely reflecting higher-than-expected commissions or travel costs tied to Q1 activity.

### Under Budget
- **Engineering** delivered the largest savings at **$2,000 (4.0%) under budget**, possibly due to open headcount or delayed tooling purchases.
- **HR** came in **$800 (5.3%) under budget**, a minor positive variance.
- **Operations** was nearly on target, just **$200 (1.0%) under budget**.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Investigate Marketing overspend** — a 25% overrun warrants a review of approved campaigns vs. actuals and tighter approval controls going forward.
2. **Reforecast Sales** — if the pipeline remains strong, the Sales budget may need upward revision for Q2/Q3.
3. **Re-deploy Engineering savings** — consider whether underspend reflects delayed hiring; if so, model headcount timing into Q2 forecasts.
4. **Overall position is manageable** — the $1,000 net overrun (0.8%) is within a normal tolerance range, but Marketing trends should be monitored closely.
