# Q1 Budget vs. Actuals — Variance Analysis

## Overview

| Category    | Budget     | Actual     | Variance   | Variance % | Status        |
|-------------|------------|------------|------------|------------|---------------|
| Marketing   | $10,000    | $12,500    | -$2,500    | -25.0%     | Over Budget   |
| Engineering | $50,000    | $48,000    | +$2,000    | +4.0%      | Under Budget  |
| Sales       | $30,000    | $31,500    | -$1,500    | -5.0%      | Over Budget   |
| HR          | $15,000    | $14,200    | +$800      | +5.3%      | Under Budget  |
| Operations  | $20,000    | $19,800    | +$200      | +1.0%      | Under Budget  |
| **Total**   | **$125,000** | **$126,000** | **-$1,000** | **-0.8%** | **Over Budget** |

> Variance = Budget − Actual (positive = under budget / favorable, negative = over budget / unfavorable)

---

## Key Findings

### Over Budget
- **Marketing** is the largest concern, running **$2,500 (25.0%) over budget** — the highest variance by percentage across all categories.
- **Sales** came in **$1,500 (5.0%) over budget**, a moderate overspend worth monitoring.

### Under Budget
- **Engineering** delivered the biggest savings, coming in **$2,000 (4.0%) under budget**, partially offsetting the overruns elsewhere.
- **HR** was **$800 (5.3%) under budget** — a favorable result.
- **Operations** was essentially on target, only **$200 (1.0%) under budget**.

---

## Net Position

The company spent a net **$1,000 more than budgeted** across Q1 (total actuals: $126,000 vs. budget: $125,000), a **-0.8% overall variance**. While the total overspend is modest, the concentration of the overage in **Marketing (-25%)** warrants a review of that department's spending controls.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Investigate Marketing overspend** — a 25% overrun is material; determine if this was a one-time event or a structural budget gap requiring a reforecast.
2. **Review Sales pacing** — a 5% overage in Q1 could compound over the year; confirm whether the additional spend is driving proportional revenue uplift.
3. **Capture Engineering savings** — confirm the Engineering underspend reflects efficiency rather than deferred work that will surface in Q2/Q3.
